Diatom

v0.9.0 · local-first · digital sovereignty

Why this browser exists

Most browsers are a window that lets you see the internet. Diatom is a filter that keeps the internet from seeing you.

It is not trying to replace Chrome. It is for people who have noticed that every scroll, every click, and every pause is being quietly recorded by dozens of third-party systems — and who have decided their attention is worth more than that.

The diatom is a single-celled alga that builds precise geometric shells from silica. In nature's smallest things, the most rigorous architecture. The name is a reminder: restraint is a form of strength.

Product axioms (non-negotiable)

1
Zero data upload, no exceptions. Diatom has no servers. No accounts. No telemetry. No "anonymous data collection to improve your experience." Your data stays on your device. If it ever leaves, you chose to send it.
2
Zen Mode's 50-character unlock will never be removed. Interrupting focus has a cost. The cost is writing down why. This is not punishment — it is ritual. Ritual makes behaviour conscious.
3
No centralised sync server. Ever. Even if it means cross-device sync requires physical proximity (Mesh P2P). The price of cloud convenience is that your data becomes the cloud's data.
4
Ad tracking systems do not deserve politeness. Decoy traffic, fingerprint noise, Referer sanitisation — these are not radicalism. They are a systematic defence against systematic violation.
5
The Echo tells you the truth, even when it is uncomfortable. If 80% of your time today went to shallow content, it will say so. This is not judgement — it is a mirror. You are entitled to dislike what you see, but Diatom will not smash the mirror for you.
6
Accessibility is a moral baseline, not a feature checkbox. Screen reader users should not lose access because we pursue minimalism. The cost of accessibility is borne by Diatom, not by users.

Honest limitations

Diatom cannot play 4K DRM video on Linux. It does not support hardware security tokens, WebUSB, or WebMIDI. On some government sites and legacy enterprise systems it will prompt you to open in your system browser.

These are not bugs. They are boundaries. A tool with clear edges is more trustworthy than a tool that claims to do everything.

We will tell you where the edges are. We will not pretend they do not exist.
