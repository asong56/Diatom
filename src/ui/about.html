Why this browser exists

Most browsers are a window that lets you see the internet. Diatom is a filter that keeps the internet from seeing you.

It is not trying to replace Chrome. It is for people who have noticed that every scroll, every click, and every pause is being quietly recorded by dozens of third-party systems — and who have decided their attention is worth more than that.

The diatom is a single-celled alga that builds precise geometric shells from silica. In nature's smallest things, the most rigorous architecture. The name is a reminder: restraint is a form of strength.

Product axioms (non-negotiable boundaries)

1
Zero data uploads, no exceptions. Diatom has no server. No account system. No telemetry. No anonymous data collection for "improving user experience". Your data stays on your device. If it leaves, that is your deliberate choice.
2
The Zen Mode 50-character gate is never removed. Breaking focus has a cost. The cost is writing down why you are breaking it. This is not a punishment — it is a ritual. Rituals make behaviour conscious.
3
No centralised sync server, ever. Even if that means cross-device sync requires physical proximity (Mesh P2P). The price of cloud convenience is that your data becomes the cloud's data.
4
Ad-tracking systems do not deserve to be treated politely. Decoy traffic, fingerprint noise, Referer sanitisation — these are not activism, they are a systematic defence against a systematic violation.
5
The Echo tells you the truth, even when it is uncomfortable. If you spent 80% of your time consuming fragmented content today, it will say so. This is not judgement — it is a mirror. You are free to dislike what you see, but Diatom will not smash the mirror on your behalf.
6
Accessibility is an ethical floor, not a feature list item. Screen reader users should not lose access because we pursue minimalism. The cost of accessibility is borne by Diatom, not by the user.

Honest limitations

Diatom cannot play 4K DRM video (Linux). It does not support bank hardware-key plugins, WebUSB, or WebMIDI. On some government sites and legacy enterprise systems, it will prompt you to open in the system browser.

These are not bugs. They are boundaries. A tool with boundaries is more trustworthy than a tool that is everywhere.

We will tell you where the boundaries are. We will not pretend they do not exist.
